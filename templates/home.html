About the Diseases

Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart, including coronary artery disease, heart rhythm problems (arrhythmias), and congenital heart defects. It is one of the leading causes of death worldwide. Major risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, obesity, smoking, diabetes, poor diet, and lack of exercise.

  • Common Symptoms: Chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, irregular heartbeat.
  • Preventive Tips: Healthy diet, regular physical activity, avoiding tobacco, managing stress, and regular health check-ups.

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a disease in which malignant (cancer) cells form in the tissues of the breast. It can occur in both men and women, though it is far more common in women. Early detection through self-exams and screenings can significantly improve the prognosis.

  • Common Symptoms: A lump in the breast, change in breast size or shape, skin dimpling, nipple discharge.
  • Preventive Tips: Regular screenings, maintaining a healthy weight, limiting alcohol, staying physically active, and knowing your family history.

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. It occurs when the pancreas doesn't produce enough insulin or the body can't effectively use the insulin it produces. There are mainly two types: Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes.

  • Common Symptoms: Frequent urination, extreme thirst, fatigue, blurry vision, slow healing of wounds.
  • Preventive Tips: Balanced diet, regular exercise, maintaining a healthy weight, monitoring blood sugar, and regular doctor visits.
